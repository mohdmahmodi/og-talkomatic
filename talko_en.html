Talkomatic™ Room Guide

Each room may have up to 5 participants. We limit the number of chatters to 5 because following 4 other chatters in "real-time" can be quite exhausting!

Within a room, your conduct is expected to be respectful of others. If you find the conduct of another chatter to be objectionable, you may "Vote Down" their behavior and if the majority of other participants do the same, that participant will be expelled from the room.

Thumbs-Up (normal state) undoes a Thumbs-Down.

Thumbs-Down signifies your disapproval of the conduct associated with the person. If the majority of the others in the room feel the same way, the person is expelled from the room immediately.

Semi-Private Room Hosts

In a semi-private room, anyone can see who is chatting from the lobby. When a lobby user wishes to join a semi-private room, the host is asked to grant access. This will be signified to the room host with a "Knock, Knock!" dialog that will appear above all of the chatters. Once a request has been acknowledged (granted or denied), the box will close and it will then be up to the requestor as to whether or not they wish to continue to enter the room.
